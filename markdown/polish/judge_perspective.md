# Judge's Perspective — Web Agents Hackathon (Feb 28 – Mar 1, 2026)

## Who I Am

I'm a judge at the Browser Use Web Agents Hackathon. I've seen a lot of hackathon projects. I know full-stack development well. I do not know your niche domain deeply unless you explain it to me clearly.

---

## How I Score You

Every team is evaluated on four dimensions. Here's how I actually think about each one:

| Criterion | Weight | What it really means to me |
|---|---|---|
| **Impact Potential** | 40% | Would anyone actually use this? Does it change something meaningful? |
| **Creativity** | 20% | Did you do something I haven't seen before? |
| **Technical Difficulty** | 20% | Did this require real engineering? Would a junior dev have built the same thing? |
| **Demo & Presentation** | 20% | Did you make me care in 4 minutes? |

Impact is worth the most. Don't forget that. It doesn't have to be "useful" in the traditional sense — but it has to feel significant.

---

## What I'm Thinking During Your Demo

You have **4 minutes**. Ideally 3 minutes of demo, 1 minute of Q&A. I'm watching for:

1. **The first sentence.** Give me a single, clear pitch. If I don't understand what you built in 10 seconds, you've already lost me.
2. **The problem.** I want to feel the pain before I see the solution.
3. **The solution.** Show me it working. Live. Don't just show slides.
4. **The "wow moment."** Every great demo has one. Find yours and lead with it.

Use the structure: **pitch → problem → solution → demo → stats (if you have them)**.

---

## What Impresses Me

- **Great UI/UX.** I can't audit your code in 3 minutes. I can see if it looks polished and works smoothly. This signals care and execution quality.
- **A real workflow automated end-to-end.** Not a toy example. Something that would take a human 20+ minutes done in seconds.
- **Knowing your user.** Tell me exactly who uses this and why they would switch to it today.
- **Scope discipline.** You mocked the parts that don't matter and built the parts that do. That's good engineering judgment.

---

## What Loses Points Fast

- Opening with "So we built a tool that uses AI to..." — too generic, start with the problem.
- Demo that only works in a happy path and breaks when I ask a follow-up question.
- Three minutes of architecture slides, one minute of rushed demo.
- Technical jargon I have to Google mid-presentation.
- A team of four where only one person speaks and the others look lost.
- Submitting late. Deadlines are final. Period.

---

## The Tracks — How I Think About Them

All teams are automatically considered for **Top 3**, **Founders Prize**, and **Most Viral**.

If you want the specialty tracks, you have to opt in manually:

- **Most Hardcore Infra** — I want to see real systems-level thinking. Not "we used Docker."
- **Best Devtool** — Does this make developers faster? Would I install it Monday morning?
- **Best Design** — Is it genuinely beautiful and intuitive, or just dark mode + rounded corners?
- **Best Use of Real-Time Data** — Is real-time actually load-bearing to the use case, or tacked on?

**Don't spread yourself thin chasing every track.** Win Top 3 first.

---

## My Honest Advice

- **Aim for first place.** Optimizing for a sponsor track when your core product is weak is a losing strategy. Build something undeniably impressive first.
- **Reserve the last 15–20 minutes before submission for demo rehearsal.** Not coding. Rehearsing.
- **Submit early.** Teams that submit at 10:00:01 AM have less rehearsal time and more stress. There's no upside to waiting.
- **2–3 person teams punch above their weight.** One frontend, one backend, one generalist. Four is usually one too many.
- **Mock non-core features ruthlessly.** Fake the data feed, the auth, the edge cases. Make the core workflow real and flawless.

---

## What Wins

A project that makes me turn to the other judges and say *"that's the one."*

It doesn't have to be technically the hardest. It doesn't have to solve world hunger. It has to:
- Feel real and usable
- Be clearly demonstrated in under 4 minutes
- Have an obvious answer to "who would use this and why"
- Look like something a small team cared deeply about

The best hackathon projects feel like they could be a real product by Monday. Build that.

---

*Good luck. I'm rooting for you to impress me.*
